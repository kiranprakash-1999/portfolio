2-Axis Gantry Stage

  • Category: Project
  • Members: Kiran Prakash, Sophie Kenny
  • Project Timeline: 3 Weeks; November, 2019

A 2-Axis gantry stage with 1 DC motor (w/ encoder) and 1 stepper. Uses a MSP430 MCU.

Designed all of the firmware, GUIs, electronics and assembly of a 2-axis stage to draw lines designed by the user. The GUI was a Windows form that took user input for coordinates and and outputted plots of the velocity and position of the motors. The GUI also communicated with the stage's MCU (MSP430) via UART.

The firmware contains all the control for the stage. It utilized timers and the UART module to record the encoder counts, communicate with the GUI, process packets, and to actually operate the motor and control its kinematics. One such control is to assure the motors move at the correct speeds given the orientation of the lines drawn. Additionally, a simple proportional controller was implemented to ensure the segments were of correct length and orientation.

The electronic components were assembled manually and I had much practice soldering ICs with just an iron.
