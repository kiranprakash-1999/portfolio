Fractal Mountain Modeler

  • Category: Project
  • Members: Kiran Prakash, Aman Brar
  • Project Timeline: March 2017

A program that draws realistic mountains with a fractal algorithm

Designed a GUI to draw mountains on a canvas based on a fractal algorithm. The algorithm connects a triangle's bisectors with lines to split it into 4 smaller triangles. The small triangle are then folded about each other at a partially random angle (configurable by the user). This process then repeats a number of times designated by the user. The greater the number of iterations, the greater the realism.

Extra features are also implemented to configure the drawing. There are features to animate the mountain about its verical (Y) axis, or simply to rotate it manually about any axis. Additionally, different shading algorithm are used to colour the triangles according to a light source designated by the user.

This was my first comprehensive OOP project. I learned about inheritance, polymorphism, classes, and objects, and UML.
