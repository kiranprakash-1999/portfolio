A prosthesis prototype admitted for CYBATHLON.

Our team built a prosthetic arm, operated by a pilot, for the international compeition, CYBATHLON. I was in an electrical lead, in charge of building several electrical subsystems for the arm: BMS, FSR Matrix control circuit, and actuator control circuit. It was here I initally gained practice circuit design experience, looking for off-the-shelf components or designing circuit if they were needed. The second image shows the initial BMS PCB I desiged for the arm, for a 4-cell Li-Po array. This was also where I learned much about PCB design, and Altium Designer.

As a lead, I was also in charge of recruiting and onboarding new members. Through this, I learned much about the chaos of new and small teams, especially with such lofty goals. It is important in this position to know some things about every aspect of your project, as what I worked on changed rapidly. Still, I met a lot of great people there and made a lot of new friends!
